Armenian BBQ recipe

Description

This is probably the national dish of Armenia – meat marinated and barbequed, served with delicious crispy potatoes, onion rings and bread.

Ingredients

  • 8 chops, pork or lamb
  • 2 onions, minced finely in a food processor
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp hot paprika
  • 20-30 basil leaves, roughly torn

Steps

  1. Mix onion, seasonings and basil leaves together in a large bowl
  2. add the chops and rub the mix all over them
  3. 150 g rice
  4. 3 medium fine chopped onions
  5. 3 cloves chopped garlic
  6. 1 big bunch of coriander
  7. Marinade for at least 24 hours (48 if you can, and re-rub the meat at least once during this time)
  8. Cook over a wood-fired barbeque until done – you want plenty of charring on the outside
  9. Serve with potatoes that have been par-boiled, sliced and tossed in any remaining marinade with a little oil
  10. roasted til browned and yummy
  11. Sprinkle with salt & pepper and ta da!
  12. Serve with salad, thinly sliced red onion and bread and you have a killer bbq meal.
Armenian national Tolma recipe
Ingredients and steps
  • 500 g minced beef
    • and parsley
  • 150 g tomato sauce
  • 15 g salt
  • 3 g black pepper
  • 300 g grape leaves
  • 1 cup plain yoghurt
  • 15 g chopped mint
  • 1 clove chopped garlic for the sauce
  1. In a large bowl, mix all ingredients, except rice and grape leaves, well by hand.
  2. Add rice and mix by hand. For those who don’t eat rice for some reason , they can make it without rice!
  3. If using fresh grape leaves, cut off the stems and blanch them in boiling water first. But if you are using canned leaves, wash in hot water.
  4. Next, prep your pot. Place some grape leaves on the bottom of a medium to large pot to prevent the tolma from sticking.
  5. Now the fun part. Spread a leaf on a plate or cutting board, wrong side up and stem end towards you. Put a teaspoon of filling near the stem end. With your hands fold the sides and then roll up like a cigar.
  6. Place the rolled dolma in the pot. Layer the dolma in two or three layers.
  7. To ensure stability, place a plate over the top and fill the pot with 2 cups of water. You can add more tomato sauce for extra flavor.
  8. Heat stove to 150C, and cook for 50 minutes.
  9. Sauce: yoghurt, mint and garlic mix together. Salt and pepper to taste.

Enjoy your meal!
